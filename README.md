Utilizing data from 7,801 permanent National Forest Inventory (NFI) plots randomly distributed across 31 provincial-level administrative regions in mainland China over four inventory periods (1999–2018), this study developed a tree species recommendation algorithm designed to support afforestation planning by identifying the suitable candidate tree species for specific sites and determining suitable locations for each species. Two deep learning models, Forest-CNN and Forest-Transformer, were developed to systematically evaluate the suitability and growth potential of major tree species across diverse plot-level site conditions in China.

Forest-CNN and Forest-Transformer models were combined to build a tree species suitability recommendation algorithm. First, Forest-CNN was employed to select the top N tree species based on their probability distribution for each plot. Then, Forest-Transformer was employed to predict the growth of the selected tree species in the current plot. The tree species that achieved the maximum growth was selected as the recommended candidate species under the given plot-level site conditions.
